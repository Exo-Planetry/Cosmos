What We do?

This project uses advanced technology, including machine learning and data analysis, to discover distant planets and assess their potential to support life. It provides an easy-to-use website to share information about these planets through visual representations.

Exoplanet Detection

Exoplanet detection is the process of identifying and confirming the presence of planets outside our solar system, known as exoplanets, through various observational methods, such as transit, radial velocity, and direct imaging.

Analyzing Biosignature

Analyzing biosignatures of an exoplanet involves studying the chemical or atmospheric markers that may indicate the presence of life, such as the detection of certain gases or compounds that are indicative of biological processes..

Habitability Prediction

An exoplanet is considered habitable if it possesses conditions that could support life, including a stable and suitable environment, the presence of liquid water, and a suitable distance from its host star, often referred to as the "habitable zone."

Reports and Summaries

A report on exoplanets would be a detailed document presenting research findings and data on these celestial bodies, while a summary would provide a concise overview of the key points and discoveries about exoplanets, typically for a more general audience.
