Sun

Chemical Composition: Core: Primarily hydrogen (~74%) and helium (~24%). Trace amounts of heavier elements such as oxygen, carbon, neon, and iron. Characteristics: Fusion reactions occur in the core, generating immense energy through nuclear fusion. Life Possibilities: Not habitable, but its energy sustains life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. It has a rocky surface and experiences extreme temperature variations... Chemical Composition: Mostly silicate rock and metal. Characteristics: No atmosphere, extreme temperature fluctuations between day and night. Life Possibilities: Unlikely due to extreme temperatures and lack of atmosphere.

  • Mercury has a thin atmosphere and a predominantly rocky composition.
  • It has a very slow rotation, completing only about 1.5 rotations per orbit around the Sun.

    • Earth

    Chemical Composition: Mostly iron, oxygen, silicon, magnesium, sulfur, nickel, and calcium. Characteristics: Liquid water, diverse ecosystems, a protective atmosphere, and moderate temperatures. Life Possibilities: Supports a vast range of life forms due to its unique conditions.

    Moon of Earth

    • Luna - Known for its intense creters.
    • Theia - Formed from Earth's mantle after an impact with Mars.

    Mars

    Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known for its reddish appearance. It has a thin atmosphere, evidence of past liquid water, and a diverse landscape... Chemical Composition: Rocky surface with a thick atmosphere mainly composed of carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid. Characteristics: Extreme greenhouse effect, high surface temperatures, and surface pressure. No moons, retrograde rotation. Life Possibilities: Harsh conditions make it inhospitable for life as we know it.

    • Phobos - The larger moon of Mars with an irregular shape.
    • Deimos - The smaller moon, also irregularly shaped.

    Jupiter

    Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. It has a dynamic atmosphere with prominent cloud bands, including the Great Red Spot... Chemical Composition: Primarily hydrogen and helium. Characteristics: Extensive atmosphere with cloud bands, powerful storms (e.g., Great Red Spot). Life Possibilities: No solid surface, extreme radiation and pressure make life improbable.

    Moons of Jupiter

    • Io - Known for its intense volcanic activity.
    • Europa - Ice-covered moon with a subsurface ocean.
    • Ganymede - The largest moon in the solar system with its own magnetic field.
    • Callisto - Heavily cratered moon with a thin atmosphere.

    Saturn

    Saturn is renowned for its stunning ring system, composed mainly of ice particles and dust. The planet itself is a gas giant with a complex atmospheric structure... Chemical Composition: Similar to Jupiter, mostly hydrogen and helium. Characteristics: Iconic ring system, diverse moon system, and hexagonal storm on the north pole. Life Possibilities: Unlikely due to extreme conditions.

    Moons of Saturn

    • Enceladus - Moon with active geysers venting water vapor into space.
    • Mimas - Known for a large crater resembling the Death Star.
    • Iapetus - Exhibits a two-toned appearance with a dark leading hemisphere and a bright trailing hemisphere.

    Uranus

    Uranus is an ice giant with a unique tilt in its rotational axis, causing extreme seasonal variations. It has a faint ring system and a bluish-green appearance due to methane in its atmosphere... Chemical Composition: Primarily hydrogen and helium with traces of methane. Characteristics: Tilted axis, unique rotational pattern, icy composition. Life Possibilities: Unlikely due to extreme conditions and lack of surface features.

    Moons of Uranus

    • Miranda - Moon with unique geological features, including large fault canyons.
    • Titania - The largest moon of Uranus with varied geological features.

    Neptune

    Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and has a dynamic atmosphere with fast winds. It has a prominent dark spot and a diverse set of moons... Chemical Composition: Similar to Uranus, hydrogen, helium, and traces of methane. Characteristics: Dynamic weather patterns, the Great Dark Spot, and the fastest winds in the solar system. Life Possibilities: Unlikely due to extreme conditions and lack of a solid surface.

    Moons of Neptune

    • Triton - Moon with a retrograde orbit and active geysers spewing nitrogen gas.
